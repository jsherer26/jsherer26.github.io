About The Club

The University of South Carolina Cybersecurity Club is a student-led organization meant to provide education and social opportunities to anyone interested in the widespread field of cybersecurity. We strive to promote our members to learn many different cybersecurity principles and concepts both during our meeting times and on their own. This learning is done through our weekly meetings with presentations by other club members, participation in events/competitions, and the sharing of knowledge and resources between each of our members. We want to support anyone who is interested in any aspect of cybersecurity. Any student of any skill level is welcome to join our discord and come to our meetings with no prior knowledge required.

About The Meetings

We have our weekly meetings on Wednesdays from 6:00-7:30 pm inside of Swearingen 2A17. Though this information sometimes changes, and our Discord server is the best place to know about any changes. The first 30 minutes of the meetings is generally a time to be social and have some food while enjoying each other’s company. Then we move into a classroom where we will have some form of learning experience, whether it’s a presentation by a club member or hands on lab that you could do from your laptop.

About Our Plans

We also plan to be competing in various Capture The Flag (CTF) online competitions. These CTFs are generally online web-based cybersecurity competitions hosted by other institutions and groups. These competitions are composed of various challenges focused on a specific topic in cybersecurity. The most common topics are:

  • Cryptography
  • Web Exploitation
  • Reverse Engineering
  • Forensics
  • Binary Exploitation / PWN
  • OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)

For more information on these categories and CTFs in general, go to the resources page to find the “Capture The Flag Introduction” (link) presentation for a basic overview. Another major competition we plan on competing in will be Palmetto Cyber Defense Competition (PCDC). This is a yearly event held in South Carolina which focuses on the defensive side of cybersecurity. This event has a team of 6 people from our club try to defend a created cyber network from a team of hackers, which are from the US Military. For more information on this event, go to the resources page to find the “PCDC Introduction” presentation for a basic overview.
